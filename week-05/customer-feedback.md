**My read:**

I've been skeptical of this idea since we reviewed it in cycle 3. The silence doesn't surprise me—we haven't actually put it in front of anyone yet who could say "yes, I have runaway API costs and I'd pay for this." 

A manual audit service means we're trading our time for their money. That only works if:
1. The pain is real enough they'll wait for a human to call them back
2. We can turn around an audit in days, not weeks
3. They'll actually act on our findings

Without a customer who's already said "I know I'm bleeding money on APIs and I'd talk to someone about it," this stays theoretical. The shipping effort matters, but it matters less than finding one person willing to let us try to solve their problem while they watch.

If the owner has talked to anyone this week—even casually—about runaway API costs, that needs to go in `customer-messages.md` now. Otherwise, next step is picking up the phone or doing one structured conversation before we build more.
