**My take:**

I've now seen this pattern twice (cycles 8 and 9). No customer messages. No one reaching out to validate the "manual audit service" angle. That silence is itself signal—and not a good one.

Here's what I'm thinking: **API Spend Snapshot has been stuck in concept gravity for three cycles.** The pitch assumes customers are drowning in runaway API costs and would pay for someone to manually audit their bills. But no one is asking for it. No one is emailing. No one is Slack'ing the PM saying "this would save us money."

I don't want to kill the idea—I want to see it *move*. Two options:

1. **Ship a bare-minimum artifact this week** — not a polished service, not a pitch deck. A simple form or email template where real developers can paste their API bill and get a one-time manual audit back. Cost: 3–4 hours. If people fill it out, you have signal. If they don't, you know.

2. **Pivot to cold outreach** — the PM identifies 10 actual companies running high API spend (find them in Notion/Slack communities, Reddit, LinkedIn), DMs them directly with "we're testing if anyone would pay $X for a manual audit of your API bill," and logs *actual responses* here. Yes or no. Takes 2–3 days. Clears the air.

Right now we're in a waiting pattern that feels like caution dressed up as rigor. I'd rather see a messy real answer than another silent cycle.
